Purpose

What is the Design Sprint?

The Design Sprint is a collaborative process designed to generate innovative, worker-centered solutions to improve safety, mental health, and well-being in the workplace. This structured approach enables worker teams to address health, safety, and well-being challenges through guided activities that promote communication, mutual learning, and shared responsibility.

Design Sprint Goals

  • 🎯 Empower workers and employers to identify key workplace safety and mental health issues and root causes using a job hazard analysis.
  • 💡 Brainstorm and prioritize feasible solutions.
  • 🌱 Foster a culture of continuous improvement and a supportive climate for safety, mental health, and well-being.
  • 🔄 Support sustainable changes that enhance well-being and workplace performance.

How to Use This Guide

This guide supports facilitators in leading the Design Sprint process. It provides step-by-step instructions, toolbox talks, and other participatory activities to engage crews, along with activity worksheets for each step. The guide encourages collaborative solution development and a shared commitment to safety, mental health, and well-being.

It is recommended that two individuals act as facilitators throughout the process, with one person responsible for step organization and timekeeping.

1
Step 1 · 30 Minutes · Task Force & Crew

Identify Main Concern

To understand crew safety, mental health, and well-being concerns, and prioritize the main concern.

Expected Outcomes

  • Crew(s) identified key workplace safety, mental health, and well-being concerns.
  • Crew(s) prioritized concerns and selected one health, safety, and well-being concern for the Task Force to focus on in the Design Sprint.

Key Definition

Health, Safety, and Well-being Concern Anything that places a worker's physical and/or mental well-being at risk (head of the fish). This is where you will put the concern prioritized in Activity #2.

Preparation Tips & Materials

Physical Materials

  • Large sheet of paper (poster or flip-chart)
  • Markers

Print Handouts

  • Identify Concerns Conversation Guide
  • Prioritize Concerns Toolbox Talk

Activities

Activity 1 · Crew Identifies Concerns During One-on-One Conversations
⏱ 15 minutes
👥 Task Force & Crew
📄 Identify Concerns Conversation Guide

Goal: Ask individual crew members about their top safety, mental health, and wellness concerns.

Task: The Task Force leads 2–3 one-on-one conversations with crew members to brainstorm safety, mental health, and wellness concerns.

Activity 2 · Crew Prioritizes Concerns and Selects One Main Concern During a Toolbox Talk
⏱ 15 minutes
👥 Task Force & Crew
📄 Prioritize Concerns Toolbox Talk

Goal: Crew prioritizes and selects one main concern.

Task: The Task Force guides the crew to prioritize and select one safety, mental health, and well-being topic of most concern.

Multiple crews? If conducting multiple toolbox talks, complete these activities with each crew, then hold a brief Task Force meeting to combine and select a single concern to address.

✅ Next Steps

  • Send a reminder to the leadership team and request they participate in the last step (Step 6).
  • Prepare for the Root Cause Analysis Toolbox Talk in Step 2.
2
Step 2 · 30 Minutes · Task Force & Crew

Identify Root Causes of Main Concern

To create a shared understanding of how specific aspects of the working environment can affect health, safety, and well-being.

Expected Outcomes

  • Crews identified the root causes of the main concern selected in Step 1.

Key Definitions

Health, Safety and Well-being Concern Anything that places a worker's physical and/or mental well-being at risk (head of the fish). Place the concern prioritized in Step 1 here.
Sub-Issue A smaller aspect of the main concern (vertical spines off the fish's backbone). The Design Sprint uses six pre-designed sub-issues to keep discussion focused: Equipment/Tools, Communication, Person Power, Work Organization, Environment, and Worksite Culture.
Contributing Factors Anything on or off the job that can lead to the sub-issue (horizontal spines off the underlying issue spines). The Task Force brainstorms contributing factors for each underlying issue, trying to uncover why the underlying issue is occurring.

Fishbone Diagram Sub-Issues

⚙️ Equipment / ToolsPhysical tools, machinery, PPE availability
📣 CommunicationInformation flow, language, meetings
👷 Person PowerStaffing levels, skills, turnover
🗂 Work OrganizationSchedules, procedures, breaks
🌍 EnvironmentPhysical conditions, heat, noise, site layout
🤝 Worksite CultureNorms, leadership behavior, trust

Preparation Tips & Materials

Physical Materials

  • Large sheet of paper (poster or flip-chart)
  • Markers

Print Handouts

  • Root Cause Analysis Toolbox Talk
  • Example Fishbone Diagram

Activities

Activity 1 · Crew Identifies Root Causes During a Toolbox Talk
⏱ 30 minutes
👥 Task Force & Crew
📄 Root Cause Analysis Toolbox Talk + Example Fishbone Diagram

Task: The Task Force leads the Root Cause Analysis Toolbox Talk with the crew. The Task Force guides the crew to identify root causes of the prioritized topic using a fishbone diagram.

Goal: Crew applies a root-cause analysis to identify root causes of the prioritized concern.

✅ Next Steps

  • Task Force prepares to reconvene in Step 3 and organize information from their Toolbox Talks with crew(s).
3
Step 3 · 60–90 Minutes · Task Force Only

Defining Desired Outcomes

To select a final concern based on Toolbox Talks and to identify the desired outcome of the sprint.

Expected Outcomes

  • Finalized the job hazard analysis based on crew feedback.
  • Identified desired outcomes (short-term and long-term) of solutions to address the main concern.
  • Created a specific and measurable safety, mental health, and well-being objective.

Preparation Tips & Materials

Physical Materials

  • Large sheet of paper
  • Markers
  • Notes from Steps 1 & 2

Print Handouts

  • Job Hazard Analysis Worksheet

Activities

Activity 1 · Finalize the Job Hazard Analysis
⏱ 20 minutes
👥 Task Force
📄 Blank Job Hazard Analysis Worksheet

Goal/Task: Incorporate crew feedback into a final job hazard analysis worksheet for one selected concern.

Activity 2 · Identify Desired Outcomes
⏱ 15 minutes
👥 Task Force
📄 Completed Job Hazard Analysis Worksheet

Goal: Determine what positive long- and short-term outcomes the task force wishes to achieve.

Task: Task Force considers: "What positive outcomes do we hope to see within the short term (3 months) and the long term (6–12 months)?"

Activity 3 · Create the Major Health, Safety, and Well-being Objective
⏱ 10 minutes
👥 Task Force
📄 Completed Job Hazard Analysis Worksheet

Goal: Develop an overall statement of the desired objective.

Task: Task Force considers: "What is the overall objective of the solutions?" Should be SMART: Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Realistic, and Time-oriented.

✅ Next Steps

  • Task Force prepares to conduct the Brainstorm Solutions Toolbox Talk.
4
Step 4 · 90 Minutes · Task Force & Crew

Develop & Analyze Solutions

Develop and analyze solutions to address a safety and mental health and well-being concern.

Expected Outcomes

  • Crew(s) develop potential solutions during Toolbox Talk.
  • Analysis of each solution for scope, benefits, resources, and barriers.
  • Stronger understanding of what will be needed to successfully implement solution(s).

Preparation Tips & Materials

Physical Materials

  • Large sheet of paper (poster or flip-chart)
  • Markers
  • Notes from Steps 1 & 2

Print Handouts

  • Completed Job Hazard Analysis Worksheet
  • Brainstorm Solutions Toolbox Talk
  • Blank Solutions Worksheet + example
  • Blank Solution Analysis Worksheet + example

Activities

Activity 1 · Crews Identify Potential Solutions During Toolbox Talk
⏱ 30 minutes
👥 Task Force & Crew
📄 Brainstorm Solutions Toolbox Talk + completed JHA Worksheet

Goal: Identify potential solutions to address the selected concern.

Task: The Task Force leads an interactive Toolbox Talk (30 min) with one crew to generate solutions for the selected concern.

Activity 2 · Task Force Reconvenes to Discuss Identified Solutions
⏱ 15 minutes
👥 Task Force
📄 Solutions Worksheet

Task: Task Force reviews solutions suggested by crew members and includes them in a revised solution worksheet. Remove duplicates.

Activity 3 · Analyze Solutions
⏱ 35 minutes
👥 Task Force
📄 Completed Solutions Worksheet + Blank Solutions Analysis Worksheet

Task: Build a business case for each solution by analyzing:

  • Scope — Who will be impacted?
  • Benefits — Short- and long-term benefits of implementing the solution?
  • Resources — What resources are required?
  • Obstacles — What barriers could interfere with successful implementation?

✅ Next Steps

  • Task Force prepares for the Prioritize Solutions Toolbox Talk (Step 5).
5
Step 5 · 60 Minutes · Task Force & Crew

Finalize & Prioritize Solutions

Finalize and prioritize solutions for safety and mental health and well-being with crew members.

Expected Outcomes

  • Crews review solutions.
  • Solutions are grouped into short-term and long-term categories.
  • Five solution activities are prioritized for implementation.
  • Team is prepared to present potential solutions to leadership.

Preparation Tips & Materials

Physical Materials

  • Flip chart or white board
  • Sticky notes
  • Markers
  • Notes from Steps 1, 2, & 4

Print Handouts

  • Completed Job Hazard Analysis Worksheet
  • Completed Solutions Worksheet
  • Completed Solution Analysis Worksheet

Activities

Activity 1 · Crew Prioritizes Solutions During Toolbox Talk
⏱ 30 minutes
👥 Task Force & Crew

Goal: Solutions are prioritized.

  1. Task Force shares solutions and their analysis with the crew.
  2. Crew members group solutions into short-term (3 months) and long-term (6–12 months) categories.
  3. Task Force and crew select/prioritize 5 solutions for presentation to leadership.

Tip: Allow time for crew members to digest the information and ask questions.

Activity 2 · Task Force Prepares to Present Solutions to Leadership
⏱ 10 minutes
👥 Task Force

Goal: Prepare a presentation to share the design sprint process and rationale for the 5 selected solutions.

  1. Prepare a presentation in a format appropriate for your company (e.g., PowerPoint slides or printed outline).
  2. Determine who will share the Design Sprint overview, who will share the JHA worksheet, and who will show the full list of solutions.

Tip: Share prioritized solutions and analysis with leadership before the next session.

✅ Next Steps

  • Task Force members should remind the leadership team who were invited to Step 6 about the time and location of the meeting.
6
Step 6 · 90 Minutes · Task Force & Leadership

Select Final Solutions & Develop Implementation Plan

Secure leadership support and plan for implementation of final solutions.

Expected Outcomes

  • Senior leadership is briefed and engaged in decision-making.
  • Two to three solutions are agreed upon for implementation.
  • A detailed implementation plan is developed with the support of leadership.

Preparation Tips & Materials

Physical Materials

  • Flip chart or whiteboard
  • Markers
  • Prepared presentation

Print Handouts

  • Completed Job Hazard Analysis Worksheet
  • Completed Solutions Worksheet
  • Completed Solution Analysis Worksheet(s)
  • Blank Solutions Implementation Planning Worksheet

Activities

Activity 1 · Share Overview of Design Sprint with Leadership
⏱ 20 minutes
👥 Task Force & Leadership

Goal/Task: Share the job hazard analysis worksheet with senior leaders.

Activity 2 · Task Force Shares Solutions with Leadership
⏱ 20 minutes
👥 Task Force & Leadership
  1. Share the 5 solutions the crews prioritized and the rationale for choosing them.
  2. Discuss the feasibility of each solution being successfully implemented.
Activity 3 · Prioritize 2–3 Solutions for Implementation
⏱ 25 minutes
👥 Task Force & Leadership

Goal: Task Force members and leadership collaborate to prioritize 2–3 solutions to implement.

Task: Each member gets three votes. Votes can be cast however the member wishes. After voting, eliminate the bottom of the field. Repeat until 2–3 solutions are identified.

Activity 4 · Develop Implementation Plan
⏱ 25 minutes
👥 Task Force & Leadership
📄 Blank Solution Implementation Worksheet

Goal: Develop a detailed plan for implementing 2–3 solutions. Task Force members and leadership collaborate to create the implementation plan including actions, who is responsible, deadlines, and evaluation measures.

Handout · Step 1, Activity 1

Identify Concerns Conversations

Purpose

Brainstorm current health, safety, and well-being concerns.

Materials Needed

Something to take notes with.

Facilitator Script

Opening (1 min)

"Today we'll take a step back and look at the biggest safety, health, or well-being challenges on this job. Instead of us picking the topic, we want to hear from you. You know best what issues affect your work and your crew. We will be conducting a design sprint to address one health, safety, and well-being concern, which means anything that places a worker's physical and/or mental well-being at risk."

If using Padlet (9 min)

"To start that process, we will ask that you use this QR code to access a Padlet. Once in the Padlet, click the plus button and type in, one at a time, the top concerns you have regarding worker health, safety and well-being."

If not using Padlet (9 min)

"I'd like to spend the next few minutes brainstorming current concerns. I'll write down your health, safety and well-being concerns on this piece of paper, but I won't write your name next to the concerns."
Handout · Step 1, Activity 2

Prioritize Concerns Toolbox Talk

Purpose

Prioritize one health, safety, and well-being concern to move forward with.

Materials (Padlet)

Flyer with QR code including instructions for downloading Padlet and voting.

Materials (No Padlet)

Large sheet of poster paper and markers.

Facilitator Script

Opening (2 min)

"Previously, we spoke to several crew members about their top health, safety, and well-being concerns. Now, as a group, we are going to prioritize the safety, health, and well-being concerns."

If using Padlet (13 min)

"Now that you have typed in your concerns, review yours and others' concerns and click the thumbs up or thumbs down to vote on what you believe is the most important concern for us to address."

If not using Padlet (13 min)

"You will each be given a marker, and you can vote for the three concerns that you believe are the most important to address. You can place all three of your votes on one concern or spread it out over 2–3 different concerns. The concerns with the highest number of votes will be combined with the highest voted concerns from the other crews, and the Task Force will select one to proceed forward with."

Closing

"Thanks for speaking up. We now have a clear priority area. Next time, we'll dig into why this issue is happening."
Handout · Step 2

Root Cause Analysis Toolbox Talk

Purpose

Finalize the job hazard analysis by obtaining crew feedback on contributing factors.

Materials Needed

Example fishbone diagram; Job hazard analysis worksheet and example.

Facilitator Script

Opening (2 min)

"In a previous toolbox talk, we identified several safety, health, and well-being concerns and prioritized them. Today, let's dig into why it's happening. If we can get to the root cause, we'll know how to fix it for good."

Context Setting

"The concern we will be addressing is [fill in the concern that the task force selected]. Today, we will spend some time digging into why this concern is happening. We would like you to share what you believe are the contributing factors for each of the sub-issues. As you do this, we ask you to think of this from a Total Worker Health perspective — what are the things on and off the job that contribute to the sub-issue of our concern."

Brainstorm (28 min)

"Let's think of the contributing factors to this concern across 6 sub-issues: Equipment/Tools, Communication, Person Power, Work Organization, Environment, and Worksite Culture. What are the contributing factors in the area of ______?"

Closing

"Great work digging deeper. We now know the reasons behind this concern. Next, we'll focus on generating solutions in future toolbox talks."
Handout · Step 4

Brainstorm Solutions Toolbox Talk

Purpose

Brainstorm potential solutions that address the root causes of the concern.

Materials Needed

3 flip chart papers labeled: Contractor, Crew Level, and Individual. Completed JHA worksheet + blank solutions worksheet.

Facilitator Script

Opening (2 min)

"We've now had a few toolbox talks with crews to understand a priority safety, health, and well-being concern and the root causes of the concern. We want to now think of as many solutions as possible to address this concern. No idea is too small — sometimes the simple fixes work best."

Brainstorm Solutions (28 min)

"Today, we will brainstorm solutions at three levels: 1. Contractor Level: policy changes and facility improvements 2. Crew Level: procedures, work organization, and communication 3. Individual: personal behavior changes Remember — solutions that have the biggest impact are those that address the problem at a systemic level within the organization."

Closing

"Great ideas today. At a future toolbox talk, we'll review the list and select the top 5."
Handout · Step 5

Prioritize Solutions Toolbox Talk

Purpose

Prioritize a solution to address the health, safety, and well-being concern.

Materials Needed

Copies of completed solutions worksheet. Stickers or dot markers for voting.

Facilitator Script

Opening (2 min)

"Last time we brainstormed solutions to [insert concern]. Today we'll pick the top 5 to move forward with. The goal is to choose practical solutions that make the most impact."

Review Solutions (3 min)

Read through the brainstorm list from last session. Bring the flip chart papers so crew members can place their vote directly on the paper.

Vote / Select Solutions (25 min)

"Today, we will prioritize solutions by having each of you vote for your three most important solutions. You will do this by placing a colored dot or marking the solutions that you think would most improve the concern we are addressing. You can allocate all three votes to one solution or spread them across 1–3 different solutions."

Closing

"We now have 5 solid solutions. Thanks for building them together. Next we will analyze each solution for feasibility and share them with leadership to determine which 2–3 solutions we'll move forward with implementing on site."
